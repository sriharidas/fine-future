Preventative care

Preventative care is a safety precaution measurement taken to prevent illness of disease and other risk factors. There are three levels of prevention care

  1. Primary prevention care
  2. Secondary prevention care
  3. Tertiary prevention care

  • Primary prevention care

    • Primary prevention care deals with prevention of disease by reducing the risk factors which are under developmental stage. Generally, it is the prevention of a disease at initial stage.

  • Secondary prevention care

    • Secondary prevention care involves the process of discovering and screening risk factors of disease.

  • Tertiary prevention care

    • Tertiary prevention care involves the process of reversing or curing disease at final stage.

Long-term healthcare

Long-term healthcare provides both medical and non-medical facilities for people who are physically disabled to do their regular activities. Long-term healthcare also provides independence and improves quality of their lives.

Home healthcare

Long-term healthcare provided formally in home is also called as Home healthcare. This healthcare is offered in two ways either formally or informally.

  • Formal healthcare

    • Formal healthcare is given by some physicians or under the order of some medical professional.

  • Informal healthcare

    • In Informal healthcare people are taken care by their family member or friends.

Primary health care (PHC)

PHC refers to essential health care that is based on scientifically sound and socially acceptable methods and technology.

In PHC, Services are provided at a cost that the community and the country can afford at every stage of their development in the spirit of self-determination and self-reliance. In other words, PHC is an approach to health beyond the traditional health care system that focuses on health equity-producing social policy.

Dental service

Dental care is the maintenance of healthy teeth and may refer to: Oral hygiene, the practice of keeping the mouth and teeth clean in order to prevent dental disorders Dentistry, the professional care of teeth, including professional oral hygiene and dental surgery.

In Dental care, A general dentist is your primary care dental provider. This dentist diagnoses, treats, and manages your overall oral health care needs, including gum care, root canals, fillings, crowns, veneers, bridges, and preventive education.
