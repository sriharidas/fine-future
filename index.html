COVID-19

COVID-19 is caused by severe acute respiratory syndrome coronavirus. The virus usually moves from one person to another with small drops made when coughing or sneezing. Coronavirus can also spread when people touch a surface with the virus, and then they touch their face.

Stages

  • Covid in nose
  • Covid in throat
  • Covid in lungs

  1. Covid in nose

    2. As more and more people have begun to step out of their homes, the chances of contracting the infection have also increased exponentially. While earlier breathlessness, fever and dry cough were considered the hallmarks of COVID-19, it is presenting itself in absolutely novel ways in different people. It is important to note that while runny nose and congestion are not typical symptoms of coronavirus, some patients do report them. In mild cases of coronavirus infection, people do have a runny nose and nasal congestion, which can be mistaken for the flu or cold, in the absence of other telltale signs of the disease.

  2. Covid in throat

    3. From fever to an itchy cough and body pain, COVID-19 symptoms are way too similar to that of a cold or flu. However, experts have repeatedly said that the earliest symptoms a person has can alert them about an active infection, including a sore throat. Having a prickly, itchy throat, which could also feel like you have experienced some swelling can be indicators of a sore throat. It is also one of the most commonly experienced symptoms in a COVID-19 infection, seen in over 52% cases globally.

  3. Covid in lungs

    4. COVID-19, the disease caused by the new coronavirus, can cause lung complications such as pneumonia and, in the most severe cases, acute respiratory distress syndrome, or ARDS. Sepsis, another possible complication of COVID-19, can also cause lasting harm to the lungs and other organs

symptoms

Some of the major symptoms of COVID-19 are:

  • Itchy throat
  • Dry throat
  • Dry cough
  • High temperature
  • Shortness of breath
  • Loss of smell

Prevention of COVID-19

  • Wash your hands frequently and carefully
  • Avoid touching your face
  • Don’t share personal items
  • Cover your mouth and nose when you cough and sneeze
  • Clean and disinfect surfaces
  • Take physical (social) distancing seriously
  • Do not gather in groups
  • Avoid eating or drinking in public places
  • Wear a mask
